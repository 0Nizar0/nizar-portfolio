Generative AI LLM
Infrastructure, Training Process & Costs

A visual breakdown of how large language models like GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, and LLaMA are trained — from raw data collection to deployment — and what it actually costs to get there.

01 — Training Process
Step 1
🗄️
Data Collection
Web crawl, books, code, academic papers, and licensed datasets. Trillions of tokens gathered from diverse sources.
Step 2
🧹
Data Processing
Deduplication, quality filtering, toxicity removal, tokenization. Raw data becomes structured training tokens.
Step 3
🧠
Pre-Training
Next-token prediction across the entire corpus. Thousands of GPUs running for months. The model learns language patterns at scale.
Step 4
🎯
Fine-Tuning (SFT)
Supervised fine-tuning on curated instruction-response pairs. The model learns to follow instructions and behave as an assistant.
Step 5
👥
RLHF
Human trainers rank model responses. A reward model is trained on those preferences and used to further align behavior.
Step 6
🛡️
Safety & Alignment
Red-teaming, adversarial testing, guardrails, and policy alignment. Constitutional AI, RLAIF, and safety classifiers applied.
Step 7
🚀
Deployment
Model served via API infrastructure. Continuous monitoring, feedback collection, and retraining cycles begin.

05 — Key Insights
Cost is Growing Exponentially
GPT-3 cost ~$4.6M to train in 2020. GPT-4 cost an estimated $50–100M+ in 2023. Each new generation requires dramatically more compute, data, and energy — a trend that shows no signs of slowing.
Alignment is Non-Trivial
Pre-training produces a capable but unaligned model. RLHF, Constitutional AI, and safety red-teaming add significant time and cost on top of pre-training. Alignment is not a checkpoint — it's an ongoing process that continues after deployment.
Open vs. Closed is a Real Trade-off
Meta's LLaMA 2 cost roughly one-fifth of GPT-4 to train and is publicly available, enabling broad research access. Closed models like GPT-4 keep training details proprietary, making independent auditing of their data, biases, and capabilities difficult.
