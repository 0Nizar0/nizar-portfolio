Pre-Trained Model Decision Matrix

Comparing model size, accuracy, speed, and explainability across NLP/generative AI, computer vision, and tabular data — to guide informed model selection.

1

Why these trade-offs matter

Choosing a pre-trained model is rarely about picking the "best" one — it is about picking the right one for the constraints of the deployment. A larger, more accurate model is not useful if it cannot run within a project's latency or interpretability requirements.

Model size

Parameter count and memory footprint determine hardware requirements, hosting cost, and how feasible on-device deployment is.

Accuracy

Benchmark performance gives a sense of how well a model performs on its target task — but benchmark gains often come with size and cost increases.

Speed & explainability

Inference latency affects user experience; explainability affects whether stakeholders can trust and act on a model's output.

3

Recommendations by domain

The "best" model depends on what the project actually needs — raw capability, deployment constraints, or the ability to explain a decision to a stakeholder.

NLP / Generative AI

GPT-4 vs. BERT-base

Choose GPT-4 for open-ended generation, reasoning, and tasks where flexibility matters more than cost. Choose BERT-base when the task is a well-defined classification problem, latency matters, or you need to inspect the model's weights directly.

Computer Vision

MobileNetV3 vs. EfficientNet-B0

MobileNetV3 is the right call for on-device or edge deployment where every millisecond and milliwatt counts. EfficientNet-B0 is a better fit when a small accuracy improvement is worth a modest increase in compute, such as server-side inference.

Tabular Data

XGBoost as the default

For structured business data — churn prediction, pricing, risk scoring — XGBoost offers a strong combination of accuracy, speed, and built-in explainability that is hard to beat without a clear reason to look elsewhere.

Across all three domains, the pattern holds: bigger is not automatically better. The right choice depends on matching size, accuracy, and speed to the actual constraints of the deployment — and weighing how much explainability the use case demands.
